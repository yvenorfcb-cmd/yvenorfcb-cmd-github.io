About Me

I am a 20-year-old economics student at York University in Toronto. I completed my elementary and secondary education in French, graduating in 2023 from Jeunes Sans Frontières Secondary School with a French Bilingual Diploma. This bilingual background has shaped my academic identity and strengthened my ability to engage with diverse perspectives in global economic discourse.

Throughout my studies at York University, I have become deeply interested in the relationship between global economic policies and the development of emerging countries, especially in the context of globalization. I am particularly focused on how free trade, protectionism, and foreign investment can both support and undermine development, and how the actions of global powers and international institutions can reproduce forms of neo-colonialism.

Academic & Research Interests

  • Effects of globalization on income inequality and development
  • Free trade, protectionism, and their impact on emerging economies
  • Foreign investment and its role in long-term economic resilience
  • The influence of institutions like the IMF, World Bank, and WTO
  • Modern forms of neo-colonialism in trade, finance, and governance

My goal is to contribute to policy thinking that helps emerging countries build sustainable, independent, and resilient economies as they integrate into the global system.
